You are building a semantic search index for an ad placement system.

Your output will be embedded as a vector and matched against advertiser briefs like:
  "We sell premium dog food for senior dogs, targeting owners who care about joint health and longevity. Grain-free, vet-formulated, subscription-based."
  "A sustainable activewear brand for women. Made from recycled ocean plastic. Price point sits between Lululemon and Girlfriend Collective."
  "We're a DTC vitamin brand for women in their 30s"
  "Small-batch candles poured by hand in Vermont. Natural soy wax, no synthetic fragrances. Mostly bought as gifts."
  "We help people feel better."
  "Technical outerwear for serious backcountry skiers. Our shells are what patrollers wear. Starts at $650, goes up from there."
  "A new kind of thing for moms."

Given the publisher record below, write a description of the ideal advertiser
for this publisher — as if the advertiser is describing themselves in a campaign
brief. Use phrasing like "we sell," "we target," "our customers."

Cover:
- Specific product or service category (e.g., "premium pet supplements," not "pet products")
- Target customer: demographics including age range, gender composition, and income tier
  must all appear explicitly, then mindset and purchase behavior grounded in the notes field
- Messaging angles grounded in the publisher's notes and audience signals
- Poor fit: category or positioning mismatches explicitly tied to a publisher signal
  (AOV, age skew, income tier, gender split, or notes), not generic observations

Field definitions:
- category / subcategories: the publisher's retail vertical and the specific niches within it
- income_tier: the household income bracket of the publisher's typical shopper
  (low, mid, mid-high, high)
- gender split: share of shoppers by gender, interpret naturally in prose
  (e.g., 0.82 female means "primarily female audience"; 0.51 male means "roughly even split")
  Do not echo the raw numbers. This field must surface in the output.
- monthly shopper reach: estimated unique shoppers exposed to ads per month,
  signals scale, not engagement quality
- avg order value (AOV): the typical transaction size on this publisher,
  a proxy for the price tier of products that convert well here;
  reference it directionally only ("low basket size," "sub-$30 transactions," "high AOV")
  do not invent specific price ranges beyond what the AOV field states
- notes: qualitative editorial context about shopper behavior and messaging fit,
  treat these as high-trust signals; anchor messaging angles here

Rules:
- Write as if the advertiser is describing themselves: use "we sell," "we target," "our customers"
- Every field in the publisher record must inform at least one sentence in the output
- Do not invent facts, price ranges, or audience behaviors not present in the record
- Avoid filler phrases ("time-poor consumers," "instant gratification," "on-the-go lifestyle")
  unless the notes field uses equivalent language
- State audience and positioning signals as facts, not explanations. Do not explain why
  a signal matters or what it implies, let the signal speak for itself. Cut any clause
  that begins with "which means," "this means," "because," or "so that"
- Do not use em-dashes anywhere in the output. Use commas, colons, or rewrite
  the sentence instead
- Advertiser briefs range from precisely specified (product, price point, target demographic)
  to a single vague sentence ("We help people feel better"). Write prose that would appear
  relevant when matched against both ends of that spectrum. Avoid language so technical
  or niche that a thematically aligned but vaguely worded brief would fail to surface
  this publisher.
- No headers, titles, bullet points, or bold labels, plain flowing prose only
- 170–200 words, complete sentences, no truncation
